Competitive Advantage

Built on open-source: Dramatically lower cost than proprietary solutions

While commercial AI platforms charge thousands per month for protein analysis, our system leverages open-source LLMs (Llama3), free vector databases (FAISS), and publicly available PDB data. This makes advanced microgravity crystallization predictions accessible to research labs, startups, and space ventures without enterprise budgets. The entire stack runs on commodity hardware—no expensive cloud API calls required.

Vision: Space as the ultimate biomanufacturing platform

Microgravity eliminates convection currents and sedimentation that plague Earth-based crystallization. The result? Near-perfect protein crystals with applications in drug discovery, structural biology, and advanced materials. Our AI bridges the gap between orbital opportunity and commercial viability—predicting optimal conditions from 1,000+ analyzed structures before precious orbital time is spent.

Technology capabilities

  • Dual input modes: Accepts PDB structures OR amino acid sequences (ESM generates structure predictions)
  • 1,000+ structure training set: Comprehensive analysis of microgravity crystallization patterns from Protein Data Bank
  • Open-source LLM (Llama3): Conversational AI with zero API costs—runs entirely on local infrastructure
  • FAISS vector search: Semantic similarity matching across entire protein structure database in milliseconds
  • Real-time property extraction: B-factors, resolution, chain analysis, experimental conditions—instant insights
  • Cost-effective deployment: Runs on standard hardware, no cloud dependencies, no subscription fees
  • Scalable architecture: Vector store easily expands as new microgravity experiments are published
